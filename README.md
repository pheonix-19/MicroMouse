A micromouse is a small robot that is designed to navigate through a maze. The goal of a micromouse competition is to build a robot that can navigate and map out an unknown maze as quickly as possible.

Here are some key details about micromice:

Size: They are very small robots, designed to fit inside mazes with paths around 10-25cm wide.
Sensors: Micromice typically use infrared or sonar sensors to detect walls and determine their location in the maze.
Navigation: They use algorithms like wall-hugging, flood-fill, or more advanced techniques to efficiently explore and map the maze.
Mapping: As they move, micromice build a map representation of the maze in their memory.
Speed: Top micromice can navigate through a complex maze in just a few seconds by calculating the shortest path.
Autonomous: They must navigate entirely autonomously, without any external control or guidance.
Competitions: There are regional and international competitions where students and hobbyists compete with their micromouse designs.
